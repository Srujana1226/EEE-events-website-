Culture and tradition are key parts of who we are, connecting us to our communities, history, and values. They help create a sense of belonging and identity. In today’s world, where cultures can easily blend together, it’s important to promote and preserve unique customs, languages, and art forms. Celebrating cultural heritage ensures that diverse ways of life are not lost. This diversity enriches our lives, offering different perspectives that can inspire creativity and innovation. Traditions often hold valuable wisdom about living sustainably and fostering community. By participating in cultural events and sharing our traditions, we can promote understanding and respect among different groups. Overall, supporting culture and tradition is about more than just keeping the past alive; it’s about creating a vibrant world where every culture adds to the richness of humanity. Embracing our differences helps us come together and navigate life’s challenges while celebrating our unique identities.

Dandiya is a traditional folk dance from Gujarat, India,performed during Navratri (9-day Hindu festival).
Dandiya which takes place not only in Gujarat but also in the rest of India and also among the Guajarati communities living abroad.
This dance form is ancient and depicts the fight between Durga Maa and Mahishasur and is performed in pairs primarily during the auspicious time of Navratri.
People dress up in their best traditional Gujarati attire and cook some of the most delicious Gujarati traditional meals whilst offering heartfelt gratitude to the goddess and protector of the world.
This dance form promotes cultural heritage, teamwork, and social bonding while improving coordination and rhythm.
Its infectious energy makes it a beloved and iconic part of Indian culture
