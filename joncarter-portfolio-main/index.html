MICHAEL FARADAY

Michael Faraday (1791-1867) was a British chemist and physicist who revolutionized the understanding of electricity and electromagnetism. With humble beginnings as a bookbinder's apprentice, Faraday's curiosity and passion for learning drove him to become one of the most influential scientists in history. Through his groundbreaking experiments and discoveries, he formulated the laws of electromagnetic induction, developed the concept of the electromagnetic field, and invented the first electrical generators and transformers. Faraday's work laid the foundation for countless technological advancements, from power generation and transmission to communication and transportation systems. His legacy continues to inspire scientists and engineers, and his name has become synonymous with innovation and discovery.
